JJ League 37 Stats

This is a collection of League Points accumulated by individual Accounts on the top 7 Teams

It was supposed to show what Teams did or may have done to accumulate these League Points to understand strategies used

However while looking at the data and the behaviour of the Accounts, things started looking suspicious and upon further investigation it turned out that some Teams have been using cheats and glitches to gain an unfair advantage. Not every Team listed is guaranteed to be cheating

Keep in mind that under Section 6 of June's Journey's Terms of Service, the (ab)use of glitches or bugs is considered cheating and therefore a violation of the Terms of Service.
Since even the sharing of glitches is a violation, the exact way of how glitches are done will not be shared for now. However, if no action is taken by Wooga, it will be taken as "part of the game", and be shared after the league concludes, to make it a fair game for everyone

Below is a list of behaviours observed on some of these Teams. Please note, that not everything applies to every Team

Examples of glitches that have been abused
  • Serving additional drinks
  • "Double drinking" drinks
  • Collecting the Club Mystery Milestones multiple times
  • Extending Competition Time
  • Sending high amounts of bushes (notably Sherlock)
Examples of unfair play observed
  • Using additional accounts for extra drinks
  • Using additional accounts for extra League Points
These have at some point been punished by Wooga, just not this time

Additionally it could be observed that in some cases, the extra accounts, that were generally on Level 16-19, accumulated League Points at an absolutely insane speed, not shown on any other Account on any Team.
Interestingly, these accounts were generally only kept in the club, while adding League Points.
The only reasonable explanation based on the speed of these accounts, their level and their behaviour, is cheating.

Reports about this behaviour, have been sent to Wooga multiple times, by multple people, but no action has been taken

The only action that has been taken by Wooga, was to remove all League Points of 5 Accounts on Imperial Evidence. Abuse of the listed glitches on those accounts, cannot be observed in the available data, like it can on other accounts in other Teams, therefore the only explanation for this action must be the amount of League Points generated overall and possibly, how even/linear the increase is.
However the same even/linear increase can be observed on many Accounts in DALLIANCE at a higher rate of speed.
This leaves the question open why no action was taken against them, while they are currently in Rank 1. The expected action here would now have to be that their accounts face the same consequences and are set to 0 for all weeks, like it was done on Imperial Evidence.

Brief description of how to spot glitch abuse in the data

Collecting CM multiple times
Increases that are multiples of 400 (such as 400, 800, 1200) in somewhat short succession, generating about 2000 League Points. This happens due to collecting the 2nd CM Milestone of 400 League Points multiple times.
A notable example of this are multiple accounts in DALLIANCE at the start of Week 4

Double Drinking
League Points generation of about 2610 in a short timeframe. Usually there is an observable increase of 90 League Points from serving 40 + 50 Drinks, followed by a steep increase of 1260 League Points achieved by using the Drink All Button, followed by a slightly slower increase of another 1260 League Points (from drinking again), resulting in about 2610 League Points from Drinks instead of 1350
Due to additional Tasks collected, this can be a slight variation of increases in the data, but is generally still noticeable, due to the overall Points generated.
Obvious examples of this are Airon and Pitohu in DALLIANCE on April 1st in Week 5, but can be observed on many other Accounts similarily as well

Extending Competition Time
This is not as easy to see in this data, but can be observed when knowing when a competition was started and how long it should've went.
A frequent occurence was that playtimes have been extended by 10-15 minutes.

Additional Accounts
Can be easily seen when looking at the legend of the charts on the right side, as it shows the contributing accounts.
Dashed Lines show accounts not being present in the Team at a certain time, which allows to easily see which accounts have been swapped.
